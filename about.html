ARTIST - PORTRAITS & PAINTINGS

Born into a family of artists, Kanchanamala Kampli was introduced to art at a very young age. K G Somashekar, her father, a much celebrated artist and photographer par excellence, was a major artistic influence in her life. Great masters such as Michelangelo, Da Vinci, Rembrandt, Salvador Dali, Shapansky, S.M. Pandit, Kathe Kollwitz, Robert Lango also influenced her and eventually she took up the study of Fine Arts.

​

Her painting sees her draw upon a deep inspiration of Surrealists, Cubists as well as the Folk Visual Arts of India. This unique combination is vivid, dramatic, true, contemplative and portrays a great sense of maturity and balance. Haunting and poignant, each work will remain with you for one reason or another. The colours of one might speak to you while on another the bare nakedness of thought will provoke a reaction. The interesting use of textures, smooth and edgy in turns, displays her strong grasp of taste and technique. A visual treat at one level, there is more than just a visual display on show here.

​

Her pencil portraits and charcoal sketches will take your breath away. Not only are they extraordinarily faithful to the subject, but each stroke seems to carry the touch of the sensitive artist and bear witness to her poetic and insightful mastery over characterization, where light and shade get fused into speaking, moist eyes at times or make the furrows etched into the lines of another face come alive on paper! Her work is exhibited at International Art Exhibitions in Europe, North America and The Middle East by Burland Galleries, London (www.burlandgalleries.com)

​

Art lovers take pride in including her work in their personal collections. Her 3-year stint as Assistant Conservator at the Regional Conservation Laboratory, Mysore added to her growing experience. As part of her work, she undertook the restoration of Oils, Watercolours, Papers, Manuscripts, Animal Skin, Metal Objects, Coins, Kadathas and Murals.
